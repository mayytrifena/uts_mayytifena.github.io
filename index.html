Know Your Facial Skin Type

Having clean and healthy facial skin is everyone's dream. To make it happen, we need to know in advance the type of skin on our face, because different skin types, different ways of care and skin care products that must be used.

Everyone has a different facial skin type and can change with age. In addition, facial skin type can also be influenced by other factors, such as genetic factors, certain diseases, and environmental factors, such as prolonged exposure to sunlight, dust, and pollution.

Certain habits can also have an impact on changing skin types, such as smoking and consuming alcoholic beverages.

Six Types of Facial Skin

There are at least four skin types that are usually listed in the product, namely oily, dry, normal, and combination. Quoted from various sources, there are actually six types of facial skin with different types of treatment. Let's see more.

Oily Skin

Oily skin is one of the most easily recognized skin types. Someone who has oily skin within an hour or two, then their face will look shiny even if they don't use moisturizer, especially on the forehead, middle of the nose and chin.

Dry skin

People who have dry skin tend to feel their skin is very tight after washing their face, especially if they don't Use a moisturizer within an hour or two of cleansing, especially around the eyes and mouth.

Sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin is usually characterized by how it reacts to care products and factors such as the sun's UV rays. If you suffer from seasonal allergies, have eczema or have asthma, chances are you have sensitive skin.

Combination Skin

Combination skin usually refers to skin that is slightly oily in the T-zone area and dry elsewhere. However, combination skin can be acne-prone and sensitive at the same time or acne-prone and dry. Pasa case everyone has a different combination.

Normal Skin

In the world of skin care and dermatology, normal skin refers to skin that does not experience any type of excessive skin condition. In other words, you will not experience a face that is too dry, oily and does not always break out.

Acne Skin

Acne is not a problem that occurs in teenagers. Skin that is prone to breakouts tends to get clogged more easily because the thicker oil can get trapped under the follicles and can't come to the surface. Acne prone skin will also have acne regularly, not at certain times.
